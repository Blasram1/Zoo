Desert Habitat

What are desert habitats? Deserts are places that don't get much rain, and are very dry. They can be either hot places, or cold places. Because deserts don't have much water, animals that live in the desert are able to conserve water and keep their body temperature at the right level.

animal Tiger Rattlesnake

Rattlesnakes are highly specialized, venomous reptiles with large bodies and triangle-shaped heads. They are one of the most iconic groups of North American snakes due to the characteristic “rattle” found at the tip of the tail.

animal Coyote

Coyotes are medium in size. They have pointed muzzles and long, brushy tails. Their coats are usually a mixture of tan, black, and gray, but can range from black to strawberry blond. The average Eastern adult coyote weighs 30 to 35 pounds, with males being heavier than females.
